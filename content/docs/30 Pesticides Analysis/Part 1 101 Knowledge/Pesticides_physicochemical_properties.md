## Herbicides
A knowledge of physicochemical properties, that is, vapor pressure (V.p.), octanol/water partition coefficient (Kow, expressed in the logarithmic form log P), and solubility in water allows the fate and behavior of such chemicals in the environment to be predicted.


### Amides
A large variety of compounds form this group of herbicides, which have the following general formula: R1–CO–N–(R2,R3).  
<img width ="360" height= "200" src = "/docs/images/Screenshot 2022-06-26 205545.png" style ="float: left" HSPACE="10" VSPACE="10"/>
The key components of this group are the N-substituted chloroacetamides and the substituted anilides.  These herbicides are normally absorbed by shoots and roots and they are, in general, nonpersistent compounds in soil.  
<img width ="360" height= "200" src = "/docs/images/Screenshot 2022-07-07 072715.png"/>


### Benzoic Acids
This group is mainly formed by chlorinated derivatives of substituted benzoic acids.
<img width ="90" height= "50" src = "/docs/images/Screenshot 2022-06-26 205823.png" style ="float: left" HSPACE="10" VSPACE="10"/>
These compounds are especially used to control deep-rooted perennial weeds and applied as salts or esters.
<img width ="540" height= "100" src = "/docs/images/Screenshot 2022-06-26 210012.png"/>


### Carbamates
Carbamates are esters of the carbamic acid (R1–O–CO–NR2R3) and together with thiocarbamates (R1–S–CO–NR2R3) represent a broad group of herbicides.  
<img width ="360" height= "200" src = "/docs/images/Screenshot 2022-06-26 210649.png"/>

<img width ="540" height= "300" src = "/docs/images/Screenshot 2022-06-26 210822.png"/>


### Nitriles
Bromoxynil and ioxynil are the hydroxybenzonitriles used as herbicides.  
<img width ="180" height= "100" src = "/docs/images/Screenshot 2022-06-26 210222.png" style ="float: left" HSPACE="10" VSPACE="10"/>
They are formulated as salts or octanoate esters and foliage applied to control broadleaved weeds in cereals and horticultural crops.  
<img width ="540" height= "300" src = "/docs/images/Screenshot 2022-06-26 210958.png"/>


### Nitroanilines
These compounds are derivatives of 2,6-dinitroaniline.

### Organophoshorus
`Glyphosate` and glufosinate are broad spectrum, nonselective, postemergence contact
herbicides active only for foliar application.  Aminomethylphosphonic acid (AMPA) is the major degradation product of glyphosate.  
<img width ="360" height= "200" src = "/docs/images/Screenshot 2022-06-26 212112.png" style ="float: left" HSPACE="10" VSPACE="10"/>

<img width ="540" height= "300" src = "/docs/images/Screenshot 2022-06-26 212557.png"/>


### Phenoxy Acids
Phenoxy acids are a common name given to a group of compounds formed by a phenoxy radical linked to a low carbon number alkanoic acid, such as 2,4-dichlorophenoxyacetic acid (2,4-D, acetic acid) or mecoprop (propionic acid). Some herbicides of this group are formed by stereoisomers, which are commercialized as single enanthiomers or racemic mixtures.  
<img width ="360" height= "200" src = "/docs/images/Screenshot 2022-06-26 212336.png" style ="float: left" HSPACE="10" VSPACE="10"/>

<img width ="540" height= "300" src = "/docs/images/Screenshot 2022-06-26 212711.png"/>


### Pyridines and Quaternary Ammonium Compounds
The herbicide group of pyridines, also named bipyridylium, is formed by paraquat
and diquat.  
<img width ="360" height= "200" src = "/docs/images/Screenshot 2022-06-26 213336.png" style ="float: left" HSPACE="10" VSPACE="10"/>
These compounds are very strong bases because of their quaternary ammonium structures
and are rapidly adsorbed and inactivated in soil.  

<img width ="540" height= "300" src = "/docs/images/Screenshot 2022-06-26 213601.png"/>
